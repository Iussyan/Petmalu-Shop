🎓 How Petmalu Shop Works (Developer Lesson)
Building a game like this requires three main concepts to work together:

1. Autoloads (Permanent Brains)
In Godot, normally everything disappears when you switch scenes. To prevent this, we use Autoloads (GameManager and FadeOverlay).

GameManager: This script lives forever. It stores your money and your list of rescued pets. When you're in the Forest and rescue a Rabbit, the data is saved in this global "Brain."
FadeOverlay: This is a black screen that stays on top of the entire game. Because it's an Autoload, it doesn't get destroyed when the world switches to the shop interior.
2. The Cinema "Fade" Trick
When you walk into a door, we use a three-step dance:

Fade Out: The FadeOverlay plays an animation to turn the screen completely black.
Switch: While the screen is black, the code instantly swaps the "Town" scene for the "Shop" scene.
Fade In: Once the new scene is ready, it tells the FadeOverlay to become transparent again. This makes the transition feel professional and "Cozy."
3. Data-Driven Spawning
We don't actually move the "Pet" object from one scene to another. Instead:

Capture: When you rescue a pet, we save its stats (Hunger: 80, Health: 100, Type: "Dog") into a list in the GameManager.
Reconstruct: When you enter the Shop, the shop's script looks at that list and "re-creates" the pet exactly as it was. This is much more stable than trying to drag objects between levels!
